📖 Indicator Guide ▲
Score0 – 100
Composite percentile rank combining all indicators. Higher = stronger momentum vs universe.
80–100 Grade A 65–80 Grade B 50–65 Grade C <50 Grade D
RSI (14)0 – 100
Relative Strength Index. Measures speed and magnitude of recent price changes. Compares average gains vs losses over 14 days.
>70 Overbought 40–70 Neutral <30 Oversold
Stoch %K0 – 100
Stochastic Oscillator. Shows where price closed relative to its high-low range over 14 days. Useful for spotting momentum shifts.
>80 Overbought 20–80 Normal <20 Oversold
ROC 10d% change
Rate of Change over 10 days. Raw price momentum — how much the stock moved in the last 2 weeks.
>5% Strong -5% to 5% Flat <-5% Weak
1M / 3M / 6M% return
Price return over 1, 3, and 6 months. Core momentum signal — stocks with strong multi-timeframe returns tend to continue outperforming.
Positive Bullish Negative Bearish
ADX0 – 100
Average Directional Index. Measures trend strength (not direction). A high ADX means a strong trend exists — whether up or down.
>40 Very Strong 25–40 Strong <25 Weak/Ranging
MACDBUY / SELL
Moving Average Convergence Divergence. BUY = 12-day EMA crossed above 26-day EMA (bullish momentum). SELL = crossed below (weakening).
BUY Bullish cross SELL Bearish cross
BB% (Bollinger)0 – 100%+
Bollinger Band position. Where price sits within the bands. >100% means price broke above the upper band — very strong but may be extended.
>100% Above band 50–100% Upper half <50% Lower half
MAs (dots)Above / Below
Two dots: MA50 and MA200. Green dot = price is above that moving average (bullish). Red = below (bearish). Both green = healthy uptrend.
🟢🟢 Strong uptrend 🟢🔴 Mixed 🔴🔴 Downtrend
52W Hi% below peak
How far price is from its 52-week high. Stocks close to their highs show sustained buying pressure. 0% = at all-time recent high.
0 to -5% Near high -5 to -15% Pullback <-15% Extended down
ATR%% of price
Average True Range as % of price. Measures daily volatility. High ATR = big daily moves. Useful for position sizing — higher ATR = more risk per trade.
>5% High volatility 2–5% Moderate <2% Low volatility
Vol Ratio× average
5-day average volume ÷ 20-day average volume. A ratio above 1× means recent volume is above normal — confirms moves. Below 1× = low conviction.
>1.5× High interest 1–1.5× Normal <1× Below average
BrkQ (Breakout Quality)0 – 100
Composite score measuring how genuine a breakout is. Combines 3 signals: (1) price above VWAP — 40%, (2) recent volume surge vs 20-day avg — 35%, (3) price proximity to 20-day high — 25%. Higher = cleaner breakout with volume confirmation.
>70 Strong breakout 40–70 Moderate <40 Weak / Fakeout risk
VWAP%% above/below
How far price is above or below the 20-day Volume Weighted Average Price. VWAP = average price paid, weighted by volume. Price above VWAP means buyers are in control. Negative = sellers dominating.
+% Above VWAP Bullish -% Below VWAP Bearish
RS Sec (Relative Strength vs Sector)% difference
Stock's 3-month return minus the median 3-month return of all stocks in its sector. Positive = outperforming its peers. This identifies rotation winners — stocks leading their sector, not just riding a sector tailwind.
+% Outperforming sector -% Underperforming sector
OBV (On-Balance Volume)Rising / Flat / Falling
On-Balance Volume tracks cumulative volume flow. On up days, full volume is added; on down days, subtracted. A rising OBV means volume is flowing into the stock (accumulation). Falling OBV = distribution. OBV rising while price rises = healthy trend.
Rising Accumulation Flat Neutral Falling Distribution
Div (RSI Divergence)● / ○
Compares the slope of price vs the slope of RSI over the last 14 bars. Divergence = they disagree, which often signals a reversal before price acts. One of the most predictive early warning signals.
● Bullish: Price↓ RSI↑ — reversal up likely ● Bearish: Price↑ RSI↓ — reversal down likely ○ No Div — trend confirmed
