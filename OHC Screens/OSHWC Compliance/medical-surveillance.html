Exam type catalogue Configured templates
Standard templates aligned with OSHWC rules; used when saving individual exams.
Exam type Scope (systems / tests) Frequency
IME – Pre‑employment Full medical history; vision (VA, colour, night blindness), hearing/audiometry, respiratory (chest exam, X‑ray where indicated, spirometry for exposed roles), cardiovascular (BP, ECG±Echo), abdomen, neuro & balance (vertigo, reflexes), locomotor, skin, hydrocele/hernia; complete labs (CBC, glucose, lipids, LFT, RFT, stool/urine, exposure‑specific tests like δ‑ALA, manganese markers, Hb electrophoresis where mandated). Once before engagement or before role upgrade.
Annual ≥ 40 yrs Annual exam for workers ≥ 40 as per prescribed format: history, general exam, vision/hearing screen, BP, BMI, core labs (CBC, FBS/PP, urine), additional tests per risk (X‑ray, ECG, others). Once in every calendar year after 40th birthday.
Periodic – hazardous process / exposures Focus on target organs based on process: lung function tests, ILO‑graded chest X‑ray where relevant, cardiovascular, neurological, liver/kidney, skin; targeted biologic monitoring (e.g. δ‑ALA in urine for lead, special neuro tests for manganese, etc.). At least annually; more frequently if required by rule / risk assessment.
Special vision / balance roles Night blindness, colour vision (Ishihara), distant/near vision per role, equilibrium tests (Romberg, tandem gait), audiometry for high‑noise roles. At IME, then typically every 2–3 years or on indication.
Adolescent fitness (14–18 yrs) Suitability for factory or construction work; restrictions on hazardous processes, night work, work at height; certificate validity and conditions recorded. At engagement; re‑exam at least annually or as ordered.
